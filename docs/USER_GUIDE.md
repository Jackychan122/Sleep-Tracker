## Tracking Sleep

### Adding a Sleep Record

1. Click the "Sleep" tab in the sidebar
2. Click the "Add Sleep Record" button
3. Fill in the required fields:
   - **Date**: The date you went to sleep
   - **Bedtime**: What time you went to bed
   - **Wake Time**: What time you woke up
4. Optionally add:
   - **Sleep Phases**: Deep sleep, REM, and light sleep durations (if available from your tracking device)
   - **Efficiency**: Percentage of time spent asleep while in bed
   - **Notes**: Any observations or factors affecting your sleep
5. Click "Save"

### Understanding Sleep Duration

**Recommended**: 7-9 hours per night for adults

- **Less than 6 hours**: May lead to decreased performance and health issues
- **6-7 hours**: Acceptable for some, but may not be optimal
- **7-9 hours**: Optimal range for most adults
- **More than 9 hours**: May indicate other issues

### Sleep Efficiency

Sleep efficiency measures the percentage of time you're actually asleep while in bed:

- **85%+**: Excellent
- **75-84%**: Good
- **65-74%**: Fair
- **Below 65%**: Poor (consider improving sleep hygiene)

### Sleep Phases

If you have a sleep tracker that provides sleep stage data:

- **Deep Sleep**: 15-20% of total sleep is optimal
  - Important for physical recovery
  - Occurs mostly in first half of night

- **REM Sleep**: 20-25% of total sleep is optimal
  - Important for memory and learning
  - Occurs mostly in second half of night

- **Light Sleep**: 50-60% of total sleep
  - Transitional sleep stages
  - Still important for rest

## Logging Workouts

### Adding a Workout

1. Click the "Workout" tab
2. Click "Add Workout"
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Date & Time**: When you exercised
   - **Type**: Choose from running, cycling, swimming, weightlifting, etc.
   - **Duration**: How long you exercised (in minutes)
   - **Intensity**: Low, medium, or high
4. Add performance metrics (if applicable):
   - **Distance**: For cardio activities (km or miles)
   - **Weight**: For strength training (kg or lbs)
   - **Sets/Reps**: For strength training
   - **Pace**: For running/cycling (min/km)
   - **Calories**: Estimated calories burned
5. Rate your:
   - **Perceived Exertion**: 1-10 scale (how hard it felt)
   - **Energy Level**: 1-5 scale (your energy during/after)
6. Add notes if desired
7. Click "Save"

### Workout Intensity Guide

- **Low**: You can carry on a conversation easily
- **Medium**: You can talk but are somewhat breathless
- **High**: You can only say a few words at a time

## Monitoring Mood & Energy

### Tracking Mood & Energy

1. Click the "Mood" tab
2. Click "Add Mood Entry"
3. Rate your:
   - **Overall Mood**: 1 (Very Poor) to 5 (Excellent)
   - **Morning Energy**: 1 (Very Low) to 5 (Very High)
   - **Afternoon Energy**: 1 (Very Low) to 5 (Very High)
   - **Evening Energy**: 1 (Very Low) to 5 (Very High)
4. Add notes about factors affecting your mood
5. Click "Save"

### Mood Rating Guide

- **5 (Excellent)**: Feeling great, positive, energetic
- **4 (Good)**: Generally positive, minor issues
- **3 (Fair)**: Neutral, average day
- **2 (Poor)**: Low energy, negative mood
- **1 (Very Poor)**: Very low energy, depressed, anxious

### When to Track

Try to track mood at consistent times each day:
- Morning: Right after waking
- Afternoon: Mid-day (around 2-3 PM)
- Evening: Before bed

## Understanding Analytics

### Dashboard

The Dashboard provides an at-a-glance view:

- **Contribution Graph**: GitHub-style heatmap showing sleep consistency
  - Green squares = good sleep
  - Darker green = better quality
  - Gray squares = no data

- **Quick Stats**:
  - Average sleep duration
  - Average sleep quality
  - Current streak
  - Recent records

### Sleep Duration Trend

Line chart showing your sleep duration over time:
- **X-axis**: Dates
- **Y-axis**: Hours of sleep
- **Ideal Range**: Highlighted between 7-9 hours

Look for:
- Consistent patterns
- Trend direction (improving or declining)
- Weekly variations

### Sleep Phase Distribution

Pie chart showing average breakdown:
- Deep sleep (blue)
- REM sleep (green)
- Light sleep (yellow)

Use this to:
- Identify if you're getting enough deep/REM sleep
- Compare to optimal percentages

### Schedule Scatter Plot

Shows relationship between bedtime and wake time:
- **X-axis**: Bedtime
- **Y-axis**: Wake time
- **Color**: Sleep quality

Look for:
- Consistent sleep schedule
- Optimal sleep window for you

### Weekly Comparison

Bar chart comparing metrics by day of week:
- See which days you sleep best
- Identify patterns (weekday vs weekend)
- Optimize your schedule

### Correlation Analysis

Scatter plots showing relationships:
- **Sleep vs Workout Intensity**: Does harder exercise affect sleep?
- **Sleep Quality vs Mood**: Does better sleep improve mood?
- **Bedtime vs Sleep Quality**: Does earlier bedtime help?

## Using Insights

### Insight Types

#### Trend Insights

Highlight increasing or decreasing patterns:
- **Duration Trends**: Sleeping more or less over time
- **Quality Trends**: Sleep quality improving or declining
- **Efficiency Trends**: Changes in sleep efficiency

#### Anomaly Insights

Flag unusual patterns:
- **Short Sleep**: Significantly less than usual
- **Long Sleep**: Significantly more than usual
- **Poor Quality**: Much lower than your average
- **Excellent Quality**: Exceptionally good sleep

#### Correlation Insights

Show relationships:
- **Exercise & Sleep**: How workouts affect your sleep
- **Mood & Sleep**: Sleep quality impact on mood
- **Schedule & Quality**: Bedtime consistency effects

#### Recommendations

Actionable suggestions:
- **Increase Duration**: If sleeping less than 7 hours
- **Improve Consistency**: If bedtime varies widely
- **Optimize Schedule**: Suggestions for better sleep timing
- **Enhance Environment**: Tips for better sleep quality

### Using Insights Effectively

1. **Review Regularly**: Check insights at least weekly
2. **Prioritize**: Focus on high-priority insights first
3. **Take Action**: Implement one recommendation at a time
4. **Track Progress**: Monitor changes over 2-3 weeks
5. **Adjust**: Refine based on what works for you

## Managing Your Data

### Exporting Data

1. Click "Settings" tab
2. Scroll to "Data Export"
3. Click "Export All Data"
4. JSON file will download automatically

**When to Export:**
- Before clearing browser data
- For backup purposes
- To transfer to another device
- For external analysis

### Importing Data

1. Click "Settings" tab
2. Scroll to "Data Import"
3. Click "Choose File" and select your JSON backup
4. Review validation results
5. Confirm import to merge data

**Warning**: Importing will merge with existing data. Review carefully before confirming.

### Creating Backups

1. Click "Settings" tab
2. Scroll to "Data Backup"
3. Click "Create Backup"
4. Timestamped backup will download

**Recommendation**: Create backups weekly or after significant data entry.

### Restoring from Backup

1. Click "Settings" tab
2. Scroll to "Data Backup"
3. Click "Restore from Backup"
4. Select your backup file
5. Confirm restore

**Warning**: Restore will replace all current data. Export current data first if needed.

## Tips for Better Sleep

### Sleep Hygiene Basics

1. **Consistent Schedule**: Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily
2. **Dark Room**: Use blackout curtains or an eye mask
3. **Cool Temperature**: Keep bedroom around 65-68¬∞F (18-20¬∞C)
4. **Quiet Environment**: Use earplugs or white noise if needed
5. **Comfortable Bed**: Invest in a good mattress and pillows

### Before Bed

1. **Limit Screens**: Avoid blue light 1-2 hours before bed
2. **Relaxing Routine**: Read, meditate, or take a warm bath
3. **Avoid Heavy Meals**: Don't eat large meals close to bedtime
4. **Limit Caffeine**: No caffeine after 2 PM
5. **Reduce Alcohol**: Alcohol may help you fall asleep but disrupts sleep quality

### During the Day

1. **Get Sunlight**: Exposure to natural light helps regulate circadian rhythm
2. **Exercise Regularly**: But avoid intense exercise 3-4 hours before bed
3. **Limit Naps**: Keep naps under 30 minutes and before 3 PM
4. **Manage Stress**: Practice stress-reduction techniques
5. **Stay Hydrated**: But reduce fluids close to bedtime

### Track and Optimize

1. **Monitor Progress**: Use the app to track what works
2. **Experiment**: Try different routines and track results
3. **Be Patient**: Sleep improvements take time
4. **Consult Professionals**: See a doctor if sleep problems persist

## FAQ

### Q: How long should I track before seeing insights?

A: At least 7 days of data is recommended for meaningful insights. More data (2-4 weeks) provides better patterns.

### Q: What if I miss a day?

A: That's okay! The app handles gaps in data. Just get back to tracking as soon as possible.

### Q: Is my data private?

A: Yes! All data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to external servers.

### Q: Can I export my data?

A: Absolutely! Use the Export feature in Settings to download all your data as JSON.

### Q: How accurate is the sleep quality score?

A: The score is based on general sleep science guidelines. Individual needs may vary. Use it as a guide, not absolute truth.

### Q: Can I use this with my fitness tracker?

A: Currently, this app is for manual entry. You can enter data from your fitness tracker manually.
